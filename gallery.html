Proceso de Comunidades Negras de Colombia

Day 1 - Walking in the sky - the blessing of the spirit

"As we introduce our journey to Bogotá we discorvered unforgettable place. The city, with its lively streets, colorful buildings, and vibrant urban atmosphere, may seem daunting to first-time visitors. However, once you let yourself be immersed in the energy of the city, there is so much to discover. Our first trip was for paying tribute to the spirits. At Cristo Rey, we had the opportunity to participate in an indigenous ceremony to bless our travels. It was a beautiful and unforgettable experience, and we felt so grateful to be able to witness and be part of this special moment. However, it is important to note that the journey to Cristo Rey was not without its challenges. As we ascended the hill, we experienced physical difficulties due to the lack of oxygen at higher altitudes. The travel fatigue made it even more difficult, but we were determined to reach the top and see the stunning panoramic view of Bogotá. When we finally arrived at the top, the view was breathtaking. We were able to admire the city below and take unforgettable photos. The statue of Cristo Rey itself was impressive, with its right arm raised to the sky. It has become an important symbol of the city and the Catholic faith. We also visited the small chapel located below the statue, which was a peaceful and spiritual experience. Overall, a visit to Bogotá is a unique experience, and a visit to Cristo Rey on the heights of the city is an unforgettable moment that deserves to be lived. Although it can be physically challenging, the breathtaking view and the cultural significance of the statue and the indigenous ceremony make it well worth the effort."

Day 2 - The spirit of the resistance

Upon our arrival, we were taken on a city walk to explore the various artistic movements that make up the streets of Bogota. The city is full of colourful and lively street art that often reflects the pain and stress experienced by Colombian citizens. These works of art have strong political connections and provide commentary on the Colombian government's actions, referencing the injustice experienced by indigenous and Afro-Colombian communities. They also address more recent events, such as the 2016 social war regarding armed conflict in Colombia and the excessive use of firearms by police and military personnel. During the city walk, we visited a cafe that supports freedom of speech and serves as an art house where individuals can create art and express their messages. This cafe provides a safe space for people to enjoy food, music, art, and the company of like-minded individuals. Here, we engaged in conversations with several representatives of groups fighting for action in Colombia. This experience was just the beginning of the many stories, pains, understandings, and respects we would gain throughout our journey.

Day 3 - heart-to-heart with the afrocolombian community

In this context, we were introduced to the PCN, an organization that fights for the rights of Afro-Colombians. While one can read about such experiences from afar, encountering the individuals who have lived through these events face-to-face transforms the experience completely. The leaders and members of PCN are highly inspiring, and their passion and drive for change are contagious. During our visit to PCN headquarters, they explained their history, development, and key principles related to their rights to their identity, territory, and culture. These principles include identifying as Afro-Colombian due to structural racism, the right to organize, the need for space, and solidarity with other communities. Our visit to a region of Bogota, which was the product of several communities' displacement in Colombia, provided us with first-hand insight into the immense damage displacement has on these populations. We met leaders who had also experienced displacement and violence from paramilitary groups. It was an emotional experience for all of us, seeing houses with no basic amenities, and meeting people who had lost everything. The concept of government or paramilitary groups being the root of this is difficult to comprehend when coming from different political structures. However, the warmth, hospitality, and kindness of these communities were immeasurable, despite the undeniable frustration and sadness. This visit further educated us on the several acts of discrimination and violence experienced by indigenous and Afro-Colombian groups, where we learned about the long line of struggle caused by displacement. These communities not only lose their homes and areas of residence but also their cultural and religious practices, and their freedom is stripped away.

Day 4 - learn to listen is the first step on the road of knowledge

On this particular day, we were presented with an exceptional opportunity to connect with the Afrocolombian community and gain invaluable insight into their perspectives on various experiences that have impacted their community. These experiences included the pervasive issue of violence and the critical implementation of the ethnic chapter of the peace treaty. Through this experience, we found ourselves deeply enriched by the knowledge and understanding we gained. We were able to learn more about the challenges that the Afrocolombian community was facing, as well as the objectives that they were striving to achieve in their journey towards peaceful integration and cohabitation. As we spent more time with the community, we were struck by their resilience, determination, and unwavering commitment to their cause. We were inspired by their passion and dedication, and we felt privileged to have had the opportunity to learn from them. Towards the end of the day, we gathered with other groups to discuss our collective journey and present our findings to key members of the community. This was an incredible opportunity to share our insights, discuss our observations, and engage in meaningful dialogue with individuals who were equally committed to the cause of peaceful coexistence. Overall, this experience left an indelible impression on us. It reinforced our understanding of the importance of active engagement with communities who are striving to achieve a better tomorrow, and it reminded us of the transformative power of cross-cultural exchange and collaboration.
